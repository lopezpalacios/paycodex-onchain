# 05 — Legal and contractual requirements

The legal foundation for wholesale CBDC participation is the FMI rulebook plus the central bank's wholesale-CBDC participation agreement. Settlement finality, default handling, and resolution interactions are determined here.

## 1. Participation agreement

- Bilateral agreement with the central bank governing subscription, redemption, and use of wCBDC.
- Eligibility, suspension, and termination conditions.
- Liability allocations for FMI / CB-side incidents.
- Data sharing and reporting obligations.

## 2. FMI rulebook

- Comprehensive rulebook governing settlement on the DLT (e.g., SDX rulebook for Helvetia).
- Settlement-finality clauses, default-management procedures, governance over rulebook changes.
- Participant obligations: capital, operational fitness, contributions to default fund where applicable.

## 3. Settlement finality designation

- Designation under the operative settlement-finality regime (EU SFD, UK SFRs, US FMI rules, FINMA FMIA, etc.).
- Confirmation that on-chain transfers achieve legal finality at the relevant moment (often the moment of on-chain confirmation under BFT consensus).

## 4. Smart contract / template legal status

- CB-approved templates have legal effect as defined in the rulebook.
- Bank-deployed contracts (where allowed) require explicit CB approval and legal status confirmation.
- "Code is law" framing not used; rulebook prevails over on-chain outcome where divergence occurs.

## 5. Liability allocations

- FMI / CB liability for platform-level incidents.
- Participant liability for participant-side incidents (key compromise, integration failure).
- Force majeure (chain outage, cyber events, market disruption).

## 6. Cross-border participation

- Multi-CBDC platforms (mBridge, Mariana, Agorá): multi-jurisdiction participation agreements.
- Choice of law and dispute-resolution mechanisms.
- Settlement-finality recognition across jurisdictions.

## 7. Default management

- Procedures on participant default: portfolio mobilisation, cash-leg recovery, customer-side communications.
- Default fund contributions where applicable.
- Coordination with CCP and securities settlement systems.

## 8. Resolution interactions

- Bank's resolution authority's mapping of wCBDC balances under bail-in / transfer.
- Continuity-of-access provisions in the rulebook for participants in resolution.

## 9. Vendor / sub-participation

- Where the bank acts as access provider for smaller participants (sub-participation), liability and AML obligations documented.

## 10. Data and confidentiality

- Settlement-flow confidentiality obligations.
- Data-sharing constraints with the CB and other participants.
- Cross-border data transfer constraints under privacy regimes.
