# 02 — Regulatory requirements

Participation in a wholesale CBDC programme is conditioned on standing as an authorised credit institution (or eligible FMI) in the issuing central bank's jurisdiction, plus admission under the FMI rulebook.

## 1. Authorisation base

- Banking licence in the jurisdiction of the issuing central bank.
- Where the bank participates as a foreign branch / subsidiary, host-state authorisation as required.
- Eligibility criteria per the CB's wholesale-CBDC programme (typically: RTGS access already in place; minimum capital; sound prudential standing; operational fitness assessment).

## 2. Programme-specific admission

### Switzerland — Project Helvetia (SNB / SDX)
- SDX participant status (FMI rulebook).
- SIC participant status (RTGS).
- SNB onboarding for wholesale CBDC subscription.

### EU — Eurosystem (TARGET2 / TIPS / Agorá scope)
- TARGET2 / T2 participant status.
- ECB / NCB onboarding for any wCBDC pilot or live scope.
- DORA compliance for ICT and third-party risk.

### UK — Bank of England (RTGS renewal / wCBDC scope)
- RTGS / CHAPS participant status.
- BoE onboarding under FMI rulebook for any wCBDC scope.

### US — Fed (NY Fed Project Cedar / Pine / Agorá)
- Fedwire participant status.
- Fed onboarding under any wCBDC scope (currently exploratory).

### Singapore — MAS (Project Guardian / Orchid / Ubin scope)
- MAS RTGS participant status.
- MAS onboarding for wCBDC pilots.

### Cross-border platforms (mBridge, Mariana, Agorá)
- Multi-CBDC participation requires admission by each participating central bank for the relevant currency leg.
- Host-state regulatory permissions for cross-border settlement activity.
- Capital-flow controls and FX-regime compatibility per jurisdiction.

## 3. Settlement-finality regime

- Designation under the EU Settlement Finality Directive (or equivalent: UK SFRs; US Federal Reserve Regulations governing FMI; SNB FMIA).
- Finality of on-chain transfers must be legally recognised under the operative regime.

## 4. Operational resilience regime

- DORA (EU) / FFIEC / Basel Principles for FMI / CPMI-IOSCO PFMI (where the platform is a designated FMI).
- TLPT / TIBER-EU / CBEST testing per FMI scope.

## 5. Cross-border permissions

- Where the bank uses wCBDC to settle with foreign counterparties, host-state rules apply.
- Capital-flow controls (especially for emerging-market participants).
- AML/CFT and Travel Rule alignment with the destination jurisdiction.

## 6. Reporting to supervisor

- Operational risk reporting on wCBDC participation.
- Material incidents per DORA / equivalent timelines.
- Periodic returns on intraday liquidity and settlement activity.

## 7. Resolution and recovery

- Wholesale CBDC participation must integrate into the bank's recovery and resolution plan.
- Continuity-of-access during resolution: how does the bank continue settling on the DLT?
- Resolution authority's mapping of wCBDC balances under bail-in / transfer.

## 8. Cross-border tax / regulatory considerations

- Settlement on multi-CBDC platforms may have FX-regime, capital-flow, and tax-information-reporting implications.
- Document the position with tax and treasury.
