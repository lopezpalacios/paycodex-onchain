# 08 — Governance, controls, internal audit

Retail CBDC distribution is a high-visibility public-good service. Governance must reflect both the scale of the customer base and the scheme operator's expectations.

## 1. Board and committees

- Board approval of CBDC distribution strategy and risk appetite.
- Risk Committee oversight of risk appetite, KRIs, incidents.
- Audit Committee oversight of audit coverage.
- Tech / Operational Resilience Committee for ICT, DORA, change governance.
- Customer Outcomes / Conduct Committee oversight of consumer-protection commitments.
- Reporting cadence: monthly to executive committee; quarterly to board committees.

## 2. Three lines of defence

- 1LoD: business + tech + customer ops.
- 2LoD: risk + compliance independent oversight.
- 3LoD: internal audit cycle covering CBDC distribution.

## 3. Roles and responsibilities

- Product owner for CBDC distribution.
- CTO / Head of Engineering.
- CISO.
- CRO.
- CCO / MLRO.
- General Counsel.
- Head of Customer Operations.
- Head of Treasury (deposit-migration impact).
- Documented decision-rights matrix.

## 4. Policy framework

- CBDC distribution product policy.
- Scheme-engagement policy.
- Customer-protection policy.
- Sanctions and AML/CFT policies (updated for CBDC).
- Information security policy.
- Third-party risk policy.
- Incident response policy.
- Reviews at least annually.

## 5. Standards and frameworks

- ISO 27001, ISO 22301, ISO 31000.
- NIST CSF 2.0 / FFIEC handbooks where applicable.
- DORA (EU) for ICT and third-party risk.
- SOC 1 / SOC 2 for service-org controls.
- Basel Principles for Operational Resilience.

## 6. Internal audit

- CBDC distribution in audit universe.
- Annual coverage of: scheme-integration controls, AML controls, customer-protection controls, fraud / dispute handling, third-party management, regulatory reporting.
- Thematic reviews on funding flows, lost-device handling, fraud incidents.

## 7. External assurance

- Statutory financial audit covers the distributor sub-ledger.
- ISAE 3402 / SOC reports for service-org aspects.
- Penetration testing including TLPT.
- Scheme-mandated assurance reports.

## 8. Issue and incident management

- Centralised issue log, owner, due date, evidence of closure.
- Lessons-learned process feeds policy and control redesign.

## 9. Training and culture

- Role-based training: developers, customer ops, compliance, fraud.
- Mandatory annual refresh; new-joiner module.
- Vulnerable-customer training for customer-facing staff.

## 10. Decision logs

- ADRs for distribution decisions.
- Scheme rulebook adoption / amendment decisions.
- Customer-T&Cs change decisions.
