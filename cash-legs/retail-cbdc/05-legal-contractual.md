# 05 — Legal and contractual requirements

The legal foundation for retail CBDC distribution is the scheme rulebook plus the customer-facing T&Cs governing the wallet and payment service. Settlement finality, refund mechanics, and customer-protection commitments are determined here.

## 1. Scheme rulebook participation

- Comprehensive rulebook governing distribution, payment processing, fraud, dispute, settlement.
- Distributor obligations: capital, operational fitness, customer-protection minima, scheme fees.
- Governance over rulebook changes; participant input mechanisms.

## 2. Distributor agreement with the central bank / scheme operator

- Bilateral agreement with the CB / scheme operator.
- Eligibility, suspension, and termination conditions.
- Reporting and data-sharing obligations.
- Liability allocations for scheme-side incidents.

## 3. Customer-facing terms and conditions

- Wallet provisioning, funding, defunding, holding cap, reverse waterfall mechanics.
- Payment mechanics, refund and dispute processes.
- No-yield commitment.
- Privacy and data-protection terms.
- Lost-device and recovery procedures.
- Freeze / closure rights — explicit grounds.
- Force majeure (scheme outage, distributor outage).
- Governing law, jurisdiction, dispute resolution.
- Wind-down and continuity-of-access commitments.

## 4. Settlement finality

- Designation under the operative settlement-finality regime.
- Confirmation that on-chain CBDC payments achieve legal finality at scheme-defined moments.

## 5. Smart contract / programmability legal status

- Programmability features limited to scheme-permitted templates.
- Customer-side scripts (where supported) constrained by scheme rules.
- Distributor not liable for scheme-template bugs but must monitor and escalate.

## 6. Liability allocations

- Scheme operator liability for scheme-level incidents.
- Distributor liability for distributor-side incidents (wallet provisioning, integration, customer service).
- Customer liability for negligence (lost-device, credential sharing).
- Force majeure clauses.

## 7. Vendor contracts

- Scheme operator: scheme rulebook + distributor agreement.
- Mobile-app vendors: DPAs, security obligations.
- Identity / KYC providers: DPAs, breach notification, sub-processor approval.
- Sanctions feed / fraud platform vendors.
- Cloud providers: DPAs + DORA-aligned clauses.

## 8. Data protection

- DPAs with every processor handling personal data.
- Standard contractual clauses for cross-border transfers.
- DPIA documented for the CBDC channel.

## 9. Cross-border interoperability

- Multi-CBDC interoperability arrangements (later phase).
- Choice of law and dispute-resolution mechanisms.
- Settlement-finality recognition across jurisdictions.

## 10. Wind-down and exit

- Scheme-defined process for distributor exit.
- Customer-portability commitments to alternative distributors.
- Continuity-of-access during wind-down.
