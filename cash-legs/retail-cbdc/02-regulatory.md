# 02 — Regulatory requirements

Most retail CBDC designs make distribution mandatory for credit institutions of a certain size. The bank's authorisation base is its existing banking / PSP licence; the additional layer is scheme participation under the operative rulebook plus enhanced compliance and resilience expectations.

## 1. Authorisation base

- Banking licence (or PSP / e-money authorisation, depending on scheme eligibility).
- For digital euro: credit institutions in the euro area are expected to provide CBDC services as part of the legal-tender framework once enacted.
- For digital pound, e-CNY, etc.: scheme-specific eligibility criteria.

## 2. Programme-specific authorisation and admission

### EU — Digital euro
- Eurosystem scheme rulebook participation.
- Compliance with the EU Digital Euro Regulation (in legislative process).
- DORA compliance; PSD3 / PSR alignment for payment-service aspects.
- AMLD / AMLR alignment.
- GDPR alignment; Data Protection Officer involvement.

### UK — Digital pound
- BoE / FCA scheme participation as defined.
- HMT-led legal framework alignment.

### China — e-CNY
- PBoC two-tier framework participation.
- AML / counter-fraud regulation alignment.

### Bahamas / Jamaica / Eastern Caribbean / Nigeria
- Local CB rulebook and supervisory expectations.

### Cross-border interoperability (later phases)
- Host-state authorisation where the bank serves cross-border CBDC payments.
- Capital-flow controls where applicable.
- AML/CFT alignment with destination jurisdiction.

## 3. Mandatory distribution

- Where distribution is mandatory (digital euro is structured this way), the bank cannot decline. Non-distribution carries supervisory consequences.
- Service-quality minimums apply: availability, accessibility, language support, vulnerable-customer accommodations.

## 4. Holding cap enforcement

- Distributor enforces holding cap (likely EUR 3K for digital euro).
- Reverse waterfall handles overruns.
- Reporting on cap breaches and exceptions.

## 5. No-yield rule

- Most designs prohibit interest on CBDC balances or specify zero policy yield.
- Distributor cannot offer remuneration on CBDC balances.

## 6. Programmability constraints

- ECB / BoE designs *exclude* targeted-use restrictions and expiry.
- e-CNY allows more programmability under PBoC policy.
- Distributor implements only programmability features the scheme rulebook permits.

## 7. Privacy regime

- Tiered privacy: small-value payments more private; larger payments fully AML-monitored.
- Distributor must implement scheme-specified data flows.
- GDPR and equivalent privacy regimes apply.
- Data-protection-impact-assessment (DPIA) required for the CBDC channel.

## 8. Supervisor engagement

- Pre-launch dialogue with home supervisor.
- Submission of: target operating model, risk assessment, ICAAP/ILAAP impact, ICT/DORA package, AML/CFT framework, customer-protection package, governance memo, exit/wind-down plan.
- Ongoing supervisory reporting cadence.

## 9. Cross-border

- Multi-CBDC interoperability: host-state authorisation per leg.
- AML/CFT alignment with destination jurisdiction.
- Capital-flow controls per local regime.

## 10. Resolution and recovery

- CBDC distribution must integrate into the bank's recovery and resolution plan.
- Continuity-of-access for customer wallets in resolution.
- Resolution authority's mapping of distributor obligations.
