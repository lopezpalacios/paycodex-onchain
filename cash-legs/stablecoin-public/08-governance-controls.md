# 08 — Governance, controls, internal audit

Governance over a regulated stablecoin issuer combines a payments-/EMI-style governance posture with crypto-native operational concerns (multi-sig key ceremonies, smart contract upgrades, bridge governance).

## 1. Board and committees

- Board approval of strategy, risk appetite, and material decisions.
- Risk Committee oversight of risk appetite, KRIs, incidents.
- Audit Committee oversight of internal audit and external attestor / auditor reliance.
- Tech / IT or Operational Resilience Committee for ICT, DORA, change governance.
- Reserve oversight committee where regime demands.
- Reporting cadence: monthly to executive committee; quarterly to board committees.

## 2. Three lines of defence

- 1LoD: business + tech + ops own the controls.
- 2LoD: risk + compliance independent oversight.
- 3LoD: internal audit periodic assurance.

## 3. Roles and responsibilities

- Product owner / GM stablecoin programme.
- CTO / Head of Engineering.
- CISO.
- CRO.
- CCO / MLRO.
- General Counsel.
- CFO / Head of Finance.
- Head of Treasury (reserve management).
- Head of Operations.
- Documented decision-rights matrix for contract upgrades, blacklist policy, reserve composition changes, bridge changes.

## 4. Policy framework

- Stablecoin product policy.
- Smart contract change policy.
- Key management and ceremony policy.
- Direct-redeemer onboarding policy.
- Blacklist and force-transfer policy.
- Sanctions and AML/CFT policies.
- Information security policy.
- Third-party risk policy.
- Incident response policy.
- Reserve management policy.
- Reviews at least annually.

## 5. Standards and frameworks

- ISO 27001, ISO 22301, ISO 31000.
- NIST CSF 2.0 / FFIEC handbooks.
- DORA (EU) for ICT risk and third-party.
- SOC 1 / SOC 2 over service-organization controls.
- Basel Principles for Operational Resilience (where applicable).

## 6. Internal audit

- Stablecoin programme in audit universe.
- Annual coverage of: smart contract change governance, key ceremonies, AML controls, reserve management, reconciliation, incident response, third-party management, regulatory reporting.
- Thematic reviews end-to-end (mint flow, redemption flow, blacklist flow, bridge flow).

## 7. External assurance

- Statutory financial audit.
- Monthly reserve attestations.
- ISAE 3402 / SOC reports for service-org aspects.
- Smart contract audits (multi-firm, pre-launch and at every material change).
- Penetration tests including TLPT / red-team.
- Proof-of-reserves / proof-of-liability where applicable (especially Model C bridges).

## 8. Issue and incident management

- Centralised issue log, owner, due date, evidence of closure.
- Lessons-learned process feeds policy and control redesign.

## 9. Training and culture

- Role-based training.
- Mandatory annual refresh; new-joiner module.
- Speak-up and near-miss reporting culture.

## 10. Decision logs

- ADRs for architectural decisions.
- Smart contract upgrade decisions logged with rationale, audit reports, sign-offs.
- Key ceremony minutes, witnessed and stored in tamper-evident archive.
