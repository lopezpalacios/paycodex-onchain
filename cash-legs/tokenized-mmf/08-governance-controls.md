# 08 — Governance, controls, internal audit

Governance over a tokenized MMF combines fund-board governance with sponsor-level controls and tokenization-specific decisions (smart contract upgrades, key ceremonies, NAV-oracle controls).

## 1. Fund board and committees

- Fund board oversight as required by the fund domicile (e.g., independent directors for Cayman / Lux structures).
- Investment committee for portfolio decisions.
- Risk committee for fund-level risk.
- Audit committee for fund-level audit.

## 2. Sponsor governance

- Sponsor board / executive committee oversight of the tokenization programme.
- Risk and audit committees as standard.
- Tech / Operational Resilience Committee for ICT, DORA where in scope.

## 3. Three lines of defence

- 1LoD: portfolio managers, fund admin liaison, tech team.
- 2LoD: risk + compliance independent oversight.
- 3LoD: internal audit cycle covering tokenization.

## 4. Roles and responsibilities

- Fund manager / portfolio manager.
- CTO / Head of Engineering.
- CISO.
- CRO.
- CCO.
- General Counsel.
- Head of Operations.
- Custodian and fund-admin liaison.
- Documented decision-rights matrix.

## 5. Policy framework

- Tokenization programme policy.
- Smart contract change policy.
- Key management and ceremony policy.
- Investor onboarding policy.
- Sanctions and AML/CFT policies.
- Information security policy.
- Third-party risk policy.
- Incident response policy.
- Reviews at least annually.

## 6. Standards and frameworks

- ISO 27001, ISO 22301, ISO 31000.
- NIST CSF 2.0 / FFIEC where applicable.
- DORA (EU) where in scope.
- SOC 1 / SOC 2 for service-org controls.

## 7. Internal audit

- Tokenization in audit universe.
- Annual coverage of: smart contract change governance, key ceremonies, NAV-oracle controls, AML controls, reconciliation, incident response, third-party management, regulatory reporting.

## 8. External assurance

- Statutory financial audit covers the fund.
- ISAE 3402 / SOC reports for service-org aspects.
- Smart contract audits (multi-firm).
- Penetration tests.
- Proof-of-reserves where the design supports.

## 9. Issue and incident management

- Centralised issue log.
- Lessons-learned process feeds policy and control redesign.

## 10. Decision logs

- ADRs for architectural decisions.
- Smart contract upgrade decisions logged with rationale, audit reports, sign-offs.
- Key ceremony minutes.
