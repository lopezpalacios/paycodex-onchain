# 02 — Regulatory requirements

A tokenized MMF is first an MMF and second a tokenized instrument. Both regimes apply.

## 1. Authorisation base

- Asset-management licence (AIFM, UCITS ManCo, RIA, MAS CMS, SFC Type 9, FINMA FinIA, etc.).
- MMF-specific authorisation per regime.
- Where the token is offered cross-border, host-state private-placement / UCITS-passport / equivalent.

## 2. MMF regimes

### EU — MMFR
- Public-debt CNAV / LVNAV / VNAV classifications with specific eligible asset, WAM, WAL, liquidity, and stress-test rules.
- Diversification limits.
- Daily redemption with potential gating mechanisms.
- ESMA reporting.

### US — Rule 2a-7
- Government / prime / tax-exempt MMF classifications.
- Eligible assets, WAM, WAL, daily/weekly liquidity floors.
- Liquidity fees and gates per recent amendments.
- SEC reporting (Form N-MFP).

### UK — FCA MMF
- Aligned to MMFR with UK adaptations post-Brexit.
- FCA reporting.

### Singapore — MAS
- SFA / CISA framework; MAS Notice on MMFs.

### Switzerland — FINMA
- CISA-regime MMFs; FINMA supervision.
- DLT Act for ledger-based shares.

### Cayman / BVI / Luxembourg / Ireland
- Common offshore / EU domiciles for institutional MMFs; local-regulator approvals.

## 3. Tokenization-layer regulation

- DLT-specific regimes where applicable: EU DLT Pilot Regime, FINMA DLT Act, MAS Project Guardian framings, Luxembourg Blockchain Acts, Liechtenstein TVTG.
- US securities law: tokens are typically securities; offering exemptions (Reg D, Reg S) framed accordingly.

## 4. Distribution

- Direct to eligible institutional investors.
- Where via licensed exchanges, exchange's regulatory wrapper applies.
- Private-placement memos / prospectuses per regime.
- Marketing rules per host state.

## 5. Investor eligibility

- Typically institutional / accredited / professional only.
- Some products have retail offerings under specific structures.
- Eligibility verified at onboarding and re-verified on schedule.

## 6. Reporting

- ESMA / SEC / MAS / FCA / FINMA periodic reporting per MMF regime.
- DLT-regime reporting where applicable.
- AML / Travel Rule reporting per below.

## 7. Cross-border

- Distribution to jurisdictions outside the home state requires host-state mapping.
- AML/CFT alignment with destination jurisdiction.
- Tax-information reporting (CRS, FATCA, DAC8).

## 8. Resolution / wind-down

- Fund-vehicle wind-down per its constitutional documents and regime.
- Investor protections via segregated custody and bankruptcy-remote fund vehicle.
- Token-specific wind-down: deactivate contracts, finalise redemptions, archive records.

## 9. Securities law

- Tokens as securities under most regimes.
- Offering exemptions (US Reg D / Reg S; EU prospectus exemptions; UK financial-promotion regime; SG accredited-investor exemptions).
- Trading-venue requirements where secondary trading occurs.

## 10. Tax

- Fund-level tax treatment per domicile.
- Investor-level taxation (income vs. capital-gain depending on jurisdiction).
- CRS / FATCA / DAC8 reporting.
- Withholding management for cross-border distributions.
