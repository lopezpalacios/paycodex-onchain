# MiCA — Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation

EU 2023/1114. Phased application. See [[../paycodex/regulations/mica]] for incumbent context.

## Cash mgmt-relevant pillars

### Stablecoin issuance (Title III + IV)

- **EMT** (E-money Token) — single fiat, e.g., EURC, USDC (EU operations)
- **ART** (Asset-Referenced Token) — basket / non-fiat
- Both require authorization
- 1:1 reserves required, redeemable at par
- Held in segregated accounts at credit institutions

### CASP regime (Title V)

- Crypto-Asset Service Providers — exchange, custody, advisory
- Authorization required
- Conduct + governance + capital rules
- Pass-portable across EU once authorized

## Bank-side implications

- Bank issuing tokenized deposit may need MiCA consideration if token serves crypto-asset role
- Bank acting as CASP needs authorization
- Reserves for issued stablecoins held at credit institutions = bank deposit business

## Status

- Stablecoin part: live since June 2024
- CASP part: live since Dec 2024
- Transitional regime for existing CASPs through mid-2026
